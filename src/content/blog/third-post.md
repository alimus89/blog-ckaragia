Greece occupies the southern tip of the Balkan Peninsula and scatters across more than **6,000 islands** in the Aegean, Ionian, and Mediterranean seas. With a history spanning over **3,000 years**, it is recognised as the birthplace of democracy, Western philosophy, the Olympic Games, and much of Western literature and science.

## Geography

The Greek landscape is overwhelmingly mountainous; the **Pindus range** forms a backbone running north to south, while **Mount Olympus** — mythological seat of the gods — rises to 2,917 m, the country's highest point. The coastline stretches for roughly 13,676 km, making it the longest in the Mediterranean.

Key geographic facts:

| Feature | Detail |

## Ancient Heritage

Greece gave the world some of its most foundational ideas and institutions:

- **Democracy** — first practised in 5th-century BC Athens under Cleisthenes
- **Philosophy** — Socrates, Plato, Aristotle shaped Western thought
- **Theatre** — tragedy and comedy originated in Dionysian festivals
- **The Olympic Games** — held at Olympia from 776 BC
- **Historiography** — Herodotus is called the _"Father of History"_

> We are lovers of beauty without extravagance, and of wisdom without weakness.
> — <cite>Thucydides, quoting Pericles' Funeral Oration</cite>

The **Parthenon** atop the Athenian Acropolis — a 5th-century BC Doric temple dedicated to Athena — remains the most iconic symbol of Classical Greece and attracts millions of visitors each year.

## Modern Greece

After nearly four centuries of Ottoman rule, Greece gained independence in **1821–1829**, becoming the first Balkan state to do so. Modern milestones include:

1. **1896** — Athens hosts the first modern Olympic Games
2. **1981** — Accession to the European Community (now EU)
3. **2002** — Adoption of the euro
4. **2004** — Athens Olympics return to the birthplace of the Games

Today Greece is a parliamentary republic of roughly **10.4 million** people. Tourism is the economy's cornerstone, accounting for about 20 % of GDP and making Greece the **9th most visited country** in the EU.

## Culture and Cuisine

Greek culture blends ancient customs with Eastern Mediterranean flavour. Highlights include:

- **Music** — _Rebetiko_, inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Heritage list, is often called "the Greek blues"
- **Cuisine** — olive oil, feta cheese, fresh seafood, _moussaka_, and _souvlaki_ define a diet recognised by UNESCO
- **Easter** — the most important holiday, celebrated with midnight liturgy, fireworks, and spit-roasted lamb
- **Shipping** — Greece controls the world's largest merchant fleet by tonnage

## Why Visit?

With Santorini sunsets, Cretan gorges, Meteora monasteries, and Thessaloniki street food, Greece offers something for every traveller. The interplay of turquoise sea, white stone, and ancient ruins creates a landscape that has inspired art and literature for millennia.
