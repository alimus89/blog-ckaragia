Xanthi is a city in the region of Western Thrace in northeastern Greece. Built amphitheatrically at the foot of the Rhodope mountain chain and divided by the Kosynthos River, it combines a beautifully preserved **old quarter** with a modern urban centre. Locals and visitors alike call it _"the Lady of Thrace"_.

## A Layered History

Xanthi's origins reach back to the Byzantine era, with the first written reference dating to **879 AD**. Over the centuries it passed through Byzantine, Ottoman, Bulgarian, and finally Greek hands. Key dates include:

1. **1361** — Ottoman conquest; Turkish settlers arrive from Asia Minor
2. **1870** — Agencies transferred from burned Genisea; Xanthi becomes regional capital
3. **1912–1920** — Balkan Wars shift control between Bulgaria and Greece
4. **1920** — Final incorporation into the Greek state

> Xanthi experienced all the tumultuous periods of the history of Thrace — raids, disasters, ethnic conflicts, civil wars.
> — <cite>Wikipedia, "Xanthi"</cite>

## The Old Town

The historic quarter, known as _Palia Poli_, is celebrated across Greece for its distinctive architecture. Walking its narrow streets you encounter:

- **Byzantine churches** dating back centuries
- **Neoclassical mansions** built by wealthy Greek tobacco merchants in the 18th–19th centuries
- **Ottoman-era mosques** reflecting the city's multicultural identity
- The **clock tower**, built by Pomak Hadji Emin Aga in 1870

## Carnival and Culture

Xanthi is nationally famous for its annual spring **carnival**, one of the largest in Greece. Over 40 cultural associations participate, and the festivities include concerts, theatre, street games, and re-enactments of old customs. The **Old Town Festival** each September is equally popular.

| Event | When | Highlight |
| ----- | ---- | --------- |
| _Xanthi Carnival_ | **February** | Street parades and music nights |
| _Old Town Festival_ | **September** | Local crafts and traditional food |
| _Saturday Bazaar_ | **Weekly** | Open-air market at Emboriou Square |

## Economy and Education

Traditionally driven by **tobacco**, commerce, and farming, Xanthi's economy has diversified with growing tourism and a strong academic presence. The city hosts the **School of Engineering** of the Democritus University of Thrace, founded in 1974, with departments covering electrical, civil, environmental, and architectural engineering.

## A Multicultural Present

Unlike most of mainland Greece, Xanthi retains a significant **Muslim minority** — a community exempted from the 1920s Greek-Turkish population exchange. Greek Orthodox Christians and Turkish-speaking Muslims share the city, giving Xanthi a character unlike anywhere else in the country.

Notable people from Xanthi include composer **Manos Hadjidakis**, winner of an Academy Award for _Never on Sunday_, and **Archbishop Christodoulos**, former head of the Greek Orthodox Church.
